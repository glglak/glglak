## About me

Most systems do not fail because nobody drew a good diagram. They fail where technology meets legacy constraints, delivery pressure, cost, operations, ownership, and human behaviour.

I work at that intersection.

I am a Principal Solution Architect with 16+ years of experience designing and delivering enterprise software across Australia, Egypt, and the Middle East. I started as a software engineer, so my architecture work stays close to code, teams, infrastructure, and the realities of running systems in production.

My focus is building solutions that are not only scalable on paper, but also secure, operable, cost-aware, and practical to deliver.

## How I approach architecture

* Architecture is a set of deliberate trade-offs, not a collection of fashionable technologies.
* Reliability, security, operability, and cost are design inputs—not post-production fixes.
* Distributed systems should be designed around failure, not only around the happy path.
* AI systems are still software systems: they need boundaries, evaluation, monitoring, and governance.
* A design is only useful when a delivery team can understand it, build it, and operate it.
